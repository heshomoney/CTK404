ABOUT: OLUWASEUN

Is a prolific creative professional who have worked in various capacities as a graphic designer, including as an art director and pre-press graphics designer. He has experience in creating design concepts for clients, developing mock-ups, exporting files, performing color press checks to print, production & logistics. He has also created logos, prepared presentations for pitches, and reviewed designs for errors before printing.

In addition to his graphic design experience, he has volunteered in various roles as a greenhold outside the creative industry, including as a chemistry teacher at a Community High School back in Enugu, Eastern part of Nigeria, corpers liaison officer at NYSC secretariat in Enugu State, and served as peer education trainee attached to an organization in Osogbo to promote and advocacy for People Living With HIV & AIDs. Prior to moving to the United States for his master programme, He volunteered as a media officer for Rotary Club of Abuja Metro, the biggest rotary club in District 9124 where he creates advertorials and event campaigns, and designing event magazines, brochures, publications, banners, roll-ups, and mock-ups. Finally, He has also volunteered as a member of a community development scheme group, recruiting and teaching volunteers to enlightening people on the Millennium Development Goals, and as a creator of the 2016 World Mosquito Day logo.
